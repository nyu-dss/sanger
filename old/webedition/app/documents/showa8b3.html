Margaret Sanger, " [Introduction for Frank Hankins] ," [26 Feb 1929] .

Typed speech. Source: Margaret Sanger Papers, Library of Congress , LCM 139:751 .

Margaret Sanger introduced Frank Hankins' remarks at a dinner for the Birth Control Clinical Research Bureau, held at New York's Plaza Hotel. Related documents are her Opening Address and introductions for Owen Lovejoy and Henry Pratt Fairchild. Additions made by hand by Margaret Sanger.

One of the most encouraging aspects of this movement is to witness the rise of a new generation of educators and leaders of opinion. ↑(↓ College professors used to be notorious for their ignorance concerning life or anything outside their own specialty. They were hard-shelled reactionaries concerning the vital problems of humanity. ↑)↓ It is my honor now to introduce a fine representative of the new educator, a man who is courageous in his outspoken attitude concerning the fundamental ethical ↑moral↓ necessity for education in Birth Control--Dr. ↑Frank↓ Hankins--
