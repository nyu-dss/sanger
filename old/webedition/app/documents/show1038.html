Powerful forces at work.

1. Marriage leads to offspring.

2. The average healthy pair can produce and raise to the age of maturity more than two children.

3. That when they continue to multiply the next generation will be more numerous than the first.

4. That an acre of land is limited to the number of plants it can occupy.

5. That the returns from crowded territory dwindle rather than increase.

A population inhabiting any limited area must do one of two things: control its increase or get its food from wider areas. To control its number is to reduce the death rate and reduce the birth rate.

There is a limit to the quantity of food that can be grown on a given acre of land--a limited space in which plants can spread their roots in the soil--their leaves in the air, and sunlight that the returns from the land are also limited. These facts have been demonstrated over and over again at various agricultural experimental stations.

This fact explains the history of migration of peoples struggling for land and markets.

It can draw its sustenance from wider areas by migrating to those areas or by selling to the inhabitants of other areas the finished product of its indoor industries--in other words in exchange for food and raw materials.
